ZipCam Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 20, 2026

ZipCam ("we", "us", or "our") provides a photo utility app for iPhone and iPad. ZipCam helps you compress photos, batch compress multiple photos, compress to a target size, create passport photos, and geotag photos when you choose to use location-enabled features. This Privacy Policy explains what data the app can access, why that access is needed, and how your data is handled.

1. Information We Collect

ZipCam is designed to process your content primarily on-device. We do not require account sign-up, and we do not ask for personal profile details like your name, phone number, or address.

  • Camera Access: Used when you capture photos directly in the app.
  • Photos Access: Used to read images you select and save processed images back to your photo library.
  • Location Access (for geotagging): Used only when you enable geotagging features. Location data is used to attach location metadata to photos as part of that feature.
  • App Usage Data Stored Locally: Certain settings and feature preferences may be stored on your device to improve your in-app experience.

2. How We Use Information

  • To provide core features such as image compression, batch processing, target-size compression, passport photo creation, and optional geotagging.
  • To save your output in your photo library when you choose to export or save images.
  • To remember your local app preferences and improve usability across sessions.
  • To support troubleshooting when you contact us directly and share details with us voluntarily.

3. Data Sharing and Selling

We do not sell your personal information. We do not share your photos with third parties for advertising. Your image processing workflow is intended to remain local to your device unless you explicitly choose to share images yourself.

4. Data Storage and Retention

Your photos and most app data remain on your device. Since data is generally stored locally, you control retention by deleting photos from your Photos app or removing app data from your device. If you contact support by email, the information you provide in that communication is retained in your email thread for support follow-up.

5. Security

We rely on Apple platform security protections and the permission model built into iOS/iPadOS. You can manage or revoke Camera, Photos, and Location access at any time from your device settings.

6. Children’s Privacy

ZipCam is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child provided personal information to us directly, please contact us so we can review and address the issue.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy as features evolve or legal requirements change. When we make changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date on this page.

8. Contact Us

For privacy questions, support, or feedback, contact us at zipcambybg@gmail.com.

You can also submit feedback using our Feedback Form.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) - ZipCam

1. What is ZipCam?

ZipCam is a photo utility app for iPhone and iPad that helps you manage image size and output quickly. You can compress single photos, batch compress multiple photos, compress images to a specific target size, generate passport photos, and add geotags when needed.

2. How do I use ZipCam?

  • Open ZipCam and choose a feature (single compress, batch compress, target-size compress, passport photo, or geotag tools).
  • Select photos from your library or capture new photos where applicable.
  • Adjust output settings and process your images.
  • Save results to your Photos app.

3. What image formats are supported?

ZipCam supports common image formats, including JPEG, PNG, and HEIC. Supported output options can vary slightly depending on the specific feature and source image.

4. Will compression reduce quality?

Compression typically reduces file size by lowering quality to some extent. ZipCam gives you control over settings so you can choose the right balance between image quality and storage savings.

5. Where are compressed or edited images saved?

Processed images are saved to your Photos library, subject to the photo permissions you grant.

6. Why is my image not compressing?

  • Confirm the selected image format is supported.
  • Check that required Photos/Camera permissions are enabled.
  • For geotagging features, confirm Location access is enabled if location data is required.
  • Try closing and reopening the app, then retry with the same image.

7. Why is the compressed image still large?

Output size depends on the original image resolution, visual complexity, and selected settings. Try a stronger compression level or lower target size if your current result is larger than expected.

8. Is ZipCam free to use?

Yes. As of April 20, 2026, all ZipCam features are free to use and there is no subscription. We may introduce paid plans or subscriptions in the future, and if that happens we will clearly communicate those changes.

9. How do I cancel my subscription?

There is currently no ZipCam subscription to cancel. If subscriptions are introduced in the future, cancellation instructions will be provided in-app and through Apple subscription settings.
